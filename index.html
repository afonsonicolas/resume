About Nicolas Afonso

Born in Miami and raised in Miami, Colombia, Brazil, and Switzerland, Nicolas comes from a multicultural background with family roots from Spain and Costa Rica. He speaks fluent English, Spanish, Portuguese, and is midway towards German proficiency. Beyond his curiosity for diversity of culture and thought, Nicolas' overseas upbringing sparked an interest in International Relations and Communication; to understand how messages and communication impact people of different experiences.

Nicolas is currently a Master's student at Georgia Institute of Technology, studying for a Master of Science in Data Analytics. Using his growing knowledge in analytics, and having completed Bachelor's degrees in Public Relations (BS) and International Relations (BA) with a minor in Business Administration at Boston University (May '23), Nicolas wants to apply his expertise to further the purpose of a strategy, communications, and marketing team.

"As an undergraduate, I learned about different marketing and communications tactics, and I've had the chance to witness some in my internships and work experience. Now, applying the skills I'm gaining at Georgia Tech, I'm really excited to join a team that informs decision-making and evaluates marketing and communications strategies. It's exciting to find the solution to a seemingly unanswerable question - especially when that solution yields positive engagement with an audience."

Most recently, Nicolas engaged with CEOs and COOs of small to mid-size businesses as an Account Manager at Kaseya, an IT software provider. "Working in sales has provided some fundamental skills I believe will help in any collaborative environment. Among the most important, listening, asking questions, and really understanding a person's point of view before drawing up conclusions will help me become a better leader and team-mate."

Previously, Nicolas served as a crisis communications consultant at Grasso Group Communications in Boston, MA. Concurrently, he worked as a pro-bono PR manager for Service Dog Project, a charity organization that breeds, trains, and donates service animals to communities in need. Nicolas has also worked in B2B PR and business development for engineering firm, Aspen Systems (Boston, MA), where he expanded the organization's shared and owned media channels through value-driven content as well as providing strategic recommendations to diversify revenue streams.

Certified in SEO, analytics, and inbound marketing, Nicolas has previously worked in luxury PR for Müller JG Jewelry in San Jose, Costa Rica as website and marketing strategy developer. Prior, he worked in health PR as an in-house communications intern for Sandoz (São Paulo, Brazil), a global leader in generic and bio-similar Pharma.

At Boston University, Nicolas achieved the Dean's List award for six semesters. He has sat on the board of International Business Club, and has served as a chair for Model United Nations (MUN). During his time at MUN, Nicolas received six public-speaking awards in a span of ten conferences attended.

After work, Nicolas enjoys reading the news and catching up on current politics, finance, and relevant industry trends. Due to his lack of a green thumb, Nicolas satisfies his love for plants and Legos by building Lego® Botanical sets and displaying them around the house. In his free time, Nicolas is an avid skier during the winter season, and enjoys golfing and kayaking in warmer months.

Analytics for Muller JG

Muller JG is the most recognized luxury jewelry retailer in Costa Rica, having a history of over 100 years in the capital city, San Jose.

While the retailer kept a database of inventory, sales, product descriptions, store traffic, and some details about visiting customers, little analysis on the data was being conducted to aid marketing efforts.

Objectives
  • Determine customer personas based on store traffic demographics
  • Identify characteristics of fastest-selling and highest margin products
  • Evaluate yearly seasonality and multi-year trends by product type to identify growing segments
Results
  • K-Means clustering revealed 3 main audience clusters with shared characteristics; commonalities helped define GTM strategies
  • Regression models showed characteristics of most marketable products, and estimation of turn over time
  • Holt-Winters exponential smoothing revealed yearly cycles and products with growing and waning demand, informing future promotion strategies

AA Price Optimizer

*This project was undertaken for academic purposes and will not be accessible for public use beyond this portfolio.*

How much is an airline mile worth, and how do we identify a good deal when buying flights with airline miles?

According to nerdwallet, each AA mile is worth 1.7 cents on average. But did you know the average price of a mile from London to Miami is 5 cents? Or that Rio to Miami averages 2.4 cents per mile on a Thursday but 3.2 cents on Tuesdays and Sundays?

This program analyzes hundreds of thousands of flights to find the average price of a mile for any American Airlines route, on any cabin, with any number of stops desired by the user.

Using advanced multi-variate regression, factors affecting the dollar price of an airline mile can be found to calculate specific price averages for flights similar to a desired flight. By comparing this average to a desired flight, users can determine whether to buy their flight in dollars or airline miles.

Once complete, the program will have a front-end interface with visualizations and recommendations for users to decide whether to used dollars or miles to purchase an American Airlines flight.

Objectives
  • Scrape all flights within 10 months of any route within 5 minutes
  • Identify factors that impact dollars per mile for a flight
  • Use these factors to calculate the average price of a mile for flights similar to flights searched by users
  • Visualize dollar and miles prices throughout the year, and recommend a user with a payment method
Where We Are
  • Dollar scraper has a 14 minute completion time
  • Miles scraper currently being optimized to reduce run time
  • Factors affecting dollars per mile identified using Ridge Regression & ARIMA
Watch it in Action
  • Normally runs in headless mode and CSS disabled for CPU efficiency
  • Capable of running roundtrip, any or all cabins, and nonstop or with stop
  • One button to run all; separate program files per website and results are merged and analyzed by a third file

Social Media Content & Articles for Aspen

Aspen Systems is a thermal components designer and manufacturer for customers in automotive, laser, biotech, semiconductor, and education.

To improve middle-of-funnel conversion, social media content and customer success articles were created to differentiate Aspen from the competition, and show customers already evaluating thermal components why Aspen had the superior product.

Objectives
  • Understand client verticals and product features they value
  • Increase middle-of-funnel conversion through value content
  • Emphasize on Aspen Systems' value propositions and differentiators
  • Reinforce Aspen Systems' brand persona through dynamic content
Results
  • Processed and analyzed content of 400+ industry journal articles using random forest & PCA to understand value drivers of each client segment
  • Produced 5 posts per week, each week focusing on a different customer vertical informed by content modeling, competitive analysis, and client interviews
  • Increased post impressions by 60% on Instagram and LinkedIn with video customer success stories
  • Created articles focused on Aspen's superior custom work, product reliability, and supplier relationship

Website & Content for SDP

Service Dog Project (SDP) is one of few free-of-charge service animal donators to individuals with mobility issues.

They operate mostly with donations from individuals who first encounter SDP on explore.com, a website with access to cameras across the world.

SDP's cameras pointed at live feeds of their puppies and dog training. With a limited budget, but a loyal explore.com audience, SDP had lost control of their narrative to user-generated resources that acted as the main source of information.

As a PR Account Manager, I led a team of 10 account executives to improve donor relations and recontrol digital channels to increase donation conversion.

Objectives
  • Increase website traffic among loyal audience base
  • Redesign website for ease-of-use
  • Centralize and command communication to audience groups
Results
  • Centralized recorded live feeds to create a story around every puppies' journey
  • Created over 75 articles, infographics, and video summaries of recorded live feeds
  • Increased website's keyword ranking by 70%, raising traffic by 500 monthly visitors
